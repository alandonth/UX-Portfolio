EveryBite

A Mobile App UX Design

Save money, enjoy new recipes, and never let your groceries expire.

Summary

A mobile app experience designed to help users save money through reduced food waste and over purchasing.

Role

UX Researcher & Designer

Solution

A digital pantry designed to track purchased and used groceries in order to provide updated shopping lists and unique recipe opportunities for each user in order to save on money and groceries.

Research, Empathize & Define

Psychiatric Service Dog Partners primarily exists to share resources and information on the process of acquiring and owning a service dog and to be a support for those in the service dog community. When speaking with our stakeholder a few very important aspects to the redesign surfaced. The website needed to be an informational resource, a place where interested visitors were drawn to reach out to peer guidance groups, and have the ability to easily receive donations without appearing to pushy. Additionally our stakeholder wanted the design team to be sensitive to the language around people with disabilities, be attentive to accessibility issues within the site, and refrain from using any colors outside of those from their brand - kelley green, gold, and black.

Next we conducted an independent survey and interviews to gather the following information on the subject.

Nearly all participants felt they were not fully informed on the laws and regulations surrounding service animals.

60% of participants reported that their frist steps in acquiring more information about the subject would be to read online.

Only 30% of participants reported that they felt confident in the process of acquiring a service animal.

From here we gathered all the information we had on our user and began to develop a user persona. Meet Mathew Cates, am IT technician who has dealt with debilitating anxiety for several years. He is introverted and has been hesitant of acquiring a service animal due to his fear of the unknown.

With our user in mind we set out to redesign the existing PSDP website. Upon analyzing and reviewing the original site design, the design team targeted a few key issues. The homepage did not present a clear hierarchy and was full of dense text blocks overwhelming users, many fonts were either too small or transparent causing accessibility issues, and finally the presence of an unclear navigation menu left users lost and frustrated. With users looking for resources and Psychiatric Service Dog Partners looking to be their source, our team established a project driving problem statement.

How might we improve the website so that visitors are quickly able to navigate to and locate the information that they need?

Ideation & Convergence

With our user persona, user research, and problem statement in hand we began to brainstorm and converge on a few key elements establishing a new flow and feel to the site design.

It was clear that both the stakeholder and the user wanted the site to be a resource hub for the community. Additionally, the stakeholder wanted to drive involvement in their peer guidance group program. As we brainstormed it became clear what the goal of our main features were to be - ways to quickly and easily access relevant information regardless of the user's experience with service animals, and doing it all in a clean and accessible manner.

Prototyping & Testing

Next we created our updated sitemap to reflect a more accurate and easy to navigate site flow and began sketching lo-fi wireframes to be tested.

Early Prototypes

We started with a hand-drawn paper sketch, followed by an initial digital sketch. Finally we created a digital prototype for the final round of testing before applying high fidelity elements

Previous Next

After another round of guerilla testing and and iterations we were able to apply our style tile to the design and increase the fidelity. Referencing back to the conversations with our stakeholder we elected for simple, easy to read fonts along with icons that clearly represented their corresponding actions. Additionally the primary color scheme of green and yellow was maintained to remain true to the organizations brand.

Solution & Implementation

The result was a redesign that brought the user more relevant action points through a clearer hierarchy and a more cohesive flow from page to page by addressing issues found in the information arhcitecture, all while presenting a cleaner and more modern aesthetic within brand limitations.
