EveryBite

A Mobile App UX Design

Save money, enjoy new recipes, and never let your groceries expire.

Summary

A mobile app experience designed to help users save money through reduced food waste and over purchasing.

Role

UX Researcher & Designer

Solution

A mobile app concept that acts as a digital pantry designed to track purchased and used groceries in order to provide updated shopping lists and unique recipe opportunities for each user in order to save on money and groceries.

Research, Empathize & Define

From the beginning, EveryBite set out to cretae a mobile application that can help individuals and families save money on their groceries and reduce food waste. We found that young professionals and families with hectic work and life schedules were having difficulty staying on budget while simultaneously throwing away a substantial amount of expired foods. We sought to answer the question - what can EveryBite do so that young professionals and families throw away less expired groceries and stay in control of their budget, while still enjoying the food they eat? We found that answer, and this is the journey of how we got there.

We started by identifying and defining our target user. We did this by collecting survey data and conducting a number of face to face interviews. A few striking statistics appeared that confirmed our hypothesis that our users prioritize cooking at home but find themselves extremely busy and are prone to waste a significant amount of food and money. With our survey and interview data gathered we established our user persona and empathy map to help drive our design decisions.

Over 56% of participants cooked at home 5-7 days per week.

The same number of participants (56%) threw away unused groceries at least once a week.

75% of participants said their average week was either very busy or extremely busy.

User Insight

Our user has limited time and needs to maintain a diverse meal plan for their family while also remaining in their budget. In order to accomplish this, they needs to determine their meal planning based off of food consumption and food waste.

Ideation & Convergence

With our user persona, user research, and problem statement in hand we began brainstorming solutions to solve our user's needs to save money and reduce grocery waste.

We started by revisitng our research data and listing out what it was our users were searching for. We then began ideating from features that exist to features that were far out. The team then took these ideas and categorized them into groups (seen in the I Like, I Wish, What If image below) and assigned dots to which corresponded to the teams interest in each one. After there were clear favorites we took the winners and positioned them onto a feature prioritization matrix to visualize their importance to the product in respects to their feasability. With our working features in place we then wanted to see how they would play out in the real world so we created a storyboard to simulate one of our user's typical interactions with the app.

Prototyping & Testing

Next we created our user flows and prepared the lo-fi prototypes of our mobile app to be tested. Below are simplified versions of two user flows based on the happy path for each feature. Both features are designed to provide recipes, one suggests recipes based on ingredients the user already has and the other provides the user the ability to easily add any neccessary ingredients to a shopping list. The "Recipes" flow has the user choose a selected recipe and provides a list of ingredients they need that can be easily added to a shopping list. The "Pantry" flow takes the user through the steps of accessing their digital pantry allowing them to see the items they already have as well as recipes based on those items.

Solution & Implementation

After multiple rounds of testing and iterations, the result was a mobile experience design that saves users money and reduces waste by notifying the user of foods that are expiring soon, allowing the user to see recipes based on foods they've already purchased, and create shopping lists based on desired recipes that only include food items they don't already have. We call it EveryBite.
