What'sHapp

A Mobile UX Design Project For Spontaneous Travelers

Summary

A mobile app design project for the travel industry based on user research findings concluding with a digital prototype.

Role

UX Researcher & Designer

Solution

A mobile app that gives users security and confidence during the booking process while emphasizing a travel experience full of freedom and flexibility.

Research, Empathize & Define

Our research began by conducting face to face interviews in search of qualitative data as well as distributing a survey for more quantitative data. We asked questions that gave us valuable insight into the mindset of modern travelers. After we listened, we began to analyze and synthesize our data utilizing our user insight board, affinity diagram, and empathy map. It wasn't long before we discovered that our users weren't looking for a way to plan their vacations but a way to feel confident in booking a vacation without losing the freedom and flexibility of being spontaneous during their vacation.

"I'm the type of person that can plan out 60% of the trip and purposely leave 40% of it unplanned to be spontaneous. " -Vishal (interviewee)

Ideation & Convergence

After analyzing our research findings we established a user persona that best represented our common user. We discovered that people don't want their vacation to feel like work. They want the freedom and flexibility in their schedule to explore and be spontaneous while still taking comfort in the fact that what they book will be worth the time and money spent on it. So we dove deeper into that challenge and began ideating.

From there we began to converge and identify which features were worth pursuing based on our users' wants and our current capabilities. We liked the idea of creating an app that allowed our users to be spontaneous. What if the user could embark on a trip without knowing most of the details up front? Why not, that certainly would be spontaneous.

Prototyping & Testing

After much synthesis and analysis we arrived at our main feature. In our research we found that the stresses of creating and sticking to a travel itinerary were often the reasons people didn't follow through with or enjoy their vacations. WhatsHapp gives its user the ability to pick a date and location, along with a few personal preferences, and is given an option of general travel packages. When the date of travel arrives all transportation and hotel arrangements are in place and users are given suggested events, activities, and eats that have been individually chosen to provide each user with a satisying and personal experience.

We began to protoype our design through a series of of sketches and digital prototypes from low fidelity to high. Starting with basic sketches we established and iterated on the basic feel of the app along with the primary flow. Slowly we increased the fiedlity using Adobe XD, iterating alonog the way. After we had a product ready to be tested we put it into the hands of users that represented our target demographic. We received valuable feedback and put it through another round of iterations before landing on a final solution.

Solution & Implementation
