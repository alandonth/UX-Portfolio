Psychiatric Service Dog Partners

A Responsive Web Design Project

For The South Carolina Organization - Psychiatric Service Dog Partners

Summary

A responsive web design project allowing PSDP to better serve as an information resource and community support for the psychiatric service dog community.

Role

UX Researcher & Designer

Solution

A redesigned desktop and mobile website prototype with improved information architecture, a cleaner and easier to navigate menu, and improved accessibility.

Research, Empathize & Define

Psychiatric Service Dog Partners primarily exists to share resources and information on the process of acquiring and owning a service dog and to be a support for those in the service dog community. When speaking with our stakeholder a few very important aspects to the redesign surfaced. The website needed to be an informational resource, a place where interested visitors were drawn to reach out to peer guidance groups, and have the ability to easily receive donations without appearing to pushy. Additionally our stakeholder wanted the design team to be sensitive to the language around people with disabilities, be attentive to accessibility issues within the site, and refrain from using any colors outside of those from their brand - kelley green, gold, and black.

Next we conducted an independent survey and interviews to gather the following information on the subject.

Nearly all participants felt they were not fully informed on the laws and regulations surrounding service animals.

60% of participants reported that their frist steps in acquiring more information about the subject would be to read online.

Only 30% of participants reported that they felt confident in the process of acquiring a service animal.

From here we gathered all the information we had on our user and began to develop a user persona. Meet Mathew Cates, am IT technician who has dealt with debilitating anxiety for several years. He is introverted and has been hesitant of acquiring a service animal due to his fear of the unknown.

Upon analyzing and reviewing the original site design, the design team targeted a few key issues. The homepage did not present a clear hierarchy and was full of dense text blocks overwhelming users, many fonts were either too small or transparent causing accessibility issues, and finally the presence of an unclear navigation menu left users lost and frustrated. With users looking for resources and Psychiatric Service Dog Partners looking to be their source, our team established a project driving problem statement.

How might we improve the website so that visitors are quickly able to navigate to and locate the information that they need?

Ideation & Convergence

With our user persona established we revisited our user interviews and began brainstorming over the wants and needs of our typical user. It was clear that there were some features that were high on our users' lists. For many, giving online can often be an unsettling means of involvement. We saw that the presence of testimonials and statistics from those impacted by the organization was important to our users to esetablish trust and increase involvement. Additionally, a quick link to an Amazon supplies page would be an easy way for people familiar with the website to get involved.

From here we created and iterated our updated sitemap, including a more organized information architecture to help drive users to more active involvement, and the user flows for our basic features.

Prototyping & Testing

With our sitemap established and research in hand, we began to design a lo-fi prototype to get into the hands of our users for testing. Our initial digital sketches were done mainly in greyscale to keep the focus on the primary flow of the site for testing.


After testing and iterating on our lo-fi wireframe we created a style tile to increase the fidelity and drive the project toward the finish line.

Solution & Implementation

Finally after testing and iterating we came to a solution that we are proud of. One with increased vibrance and colors to engage the user, a simplified information architecture to help the user navigate, and a clearer call to action with a range of ways for users to get involved.
