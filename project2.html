A Gift For Teaching

Responsive Web Design

For The Orlando, Florida Non-Profit Organization - A Gift For Teaching

Summary

A responsive web design project with the goal of generating more involvment for the Orlando based non-profit A Gift for Teaching.

Role

UX/UI Designer & Front End Developer

Solution

A redesigned website introducing a more vibrant color scheme, improved information architecture, and clearer opportnities for users to get involved and help A Gift for Teaching achieve their goals.

Research, Empathize & Define

To better understand who we were working with and designing for, we did some research on the issue that motivated our stakeholder - namely the lack of affordable school supplies for so many grade students in America. Below is what we found.

92.4%

of teachers in America spend $450 of their own money on school supplies for students in their classroom.

$117

The average household is expected to pay $117 for standard middle school supplies

15 Million

An estimated 15 million children in the United States cannot afford school supplies, let alone clothes or food

Additionally, our own survey data showed that the issue already had awareness with over 40% of those surveyed personally knowing anywhere from 1 to 3 persons who have been affected by this issue. With that and the fact that 75% of those surveyed prefered to donate online when involved with a charity or non-profit organization A Gift for Teaching was in a good position to advance as an organization and impact their community. Unfortunately upon reviewing their website, we found a few main issues that could prevent individuals and companies from getting involved, including

A poor information architecture with many links labeled differently but leading to the same page, a user interaction and overall site style that was not appealing and could leave visitors with feelings of distrust, and lastly a lack of a clear call to action to get involved with giving and donations.

This led us to our problem statement which drove our work. AGFT was designed to assist teachers, volunteers and donors to raise funds for school supplies in the Central Florida area. We have observed that AGFT lacked the information architecture to provide a clear call to action for a person who wants to get involved. When a person donates to AGFT, the teachers are freed up to focus on educating. How might we help a person wanting to donate to A Gift For Teaching feel more confident about the process so that teachers and students can better focus on their education? From here we began to establish our target user and further develop our user persona.

Ideation & Convergence

With our user persona established we revisited our user interviews and began brainstorming over the wants and needs of our typical user. It was clear that there were some features that were high on our users' lists. For many, giving online can often be an unsettling means of involvement. We saw that the presence of testimonials and statistics from those impacted by the organization was important to our users to esetablish trust and increase involvement. Additionally, a quick link to an Amazon supplies page would be an easy way for people familiar with the website to get involved.

From here we created and iterated our updated sitemap, including a more organized information architecture to help drive users to more active involvement, and the user flows for our basic features.

Prototyping & Testing

With our sitemap established and research in hand, we began to design a lo-fi prototype to get into the hands of our users for testing. Our initial digital sketches were done mainly in greyscale to keep the focus on the primary flow of the site for testing.


After testing and iterating on our lo-fi wireframe we created a style tile to increase the fidelity and drive the project toward the finish line.

Solution & Implementation

Finally after testing and iterating we came to a solution that we are proud of. One with increased vibrance and colors to engage the user, a simplified information architecture to help the user navigate, and a clearer call to action with a range of ways for users to get involved.
